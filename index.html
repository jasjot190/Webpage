üîç Introduction to Neural Networks

Neural networks are at the heart of artificial intelligence. Modeled after the human brain, these networks consist of interconnected layers of nodes (neurons) that work together to recognize patterns, make predictions, and learn from data.

They are the foundation of deep learning and power applications like speech recognition, image classification, recommendation systems, and more.

üìö Types of Neural Networks

There are many kinds of neural networks, each designed to solve a different category of problems. Let's explore two fundamental ones:

üßæ Artificial Neural Network (ANN)

ANN is the most basic form of neural network. It consists of three main types of layers: input, hidden, and output. Each neuron receives inputs, multiplies them by weights, adds bias, and passes the result through an activation function.

ANNs are used in tasks like credit scoring, spam detection, and basic image and voice recognition.

üñºÔ∏è Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)

CNNs are specialized for processing grid-like data such as images. Instead of fully connecting all neurons, CNNs use filters (kernels) to scan the image and detect features like edges, textures, and shapes.

They are the backbone of modern computer vision systems and are used in facial recognition, object detection, autonomous vehicles, and medical imaging.
